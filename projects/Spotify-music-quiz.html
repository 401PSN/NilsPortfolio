Spotify Music Quiz

CHALLENGE

The UX/UI challenge, as given by D&AD - Young Blood Awards, was to get listeners to soundtrack and share their life through Spotify. Our team was to build a feature within the app to help people easily share their favourite music and get people talking. And this is how we did it.

Duration: 48h
PARTICIPANTS Nils Andersson Project Manager & Ux Designer Hanna LarssonUx Designer Anne ObelCopywriter & Video Editor Johanna LööfDesigner & Producer
Creating a seamless Music Quiz Experience

Understanding that people are eager to party again, we wanted to create something where humans can engage with each other and get to know one and other with music in the center.

Looking for pent-up demand and needs due to covid; jobs-to-be-done sharing music; and pains & gains doing so - we found something that everybody loves to do, but only does once in a while. And we asked ourselves the question - why?

Everybody loves Music Quizzes - except one person.

Playing a Music quiz a lovely social, but the hustle of creating one; the uncle that always cheats; and spending hours decipher unreadable writing style is not as great.

A pain our team was excited to solve. Other ideas was turned downed after an intense ideating and scoring each idea in ‘prototyping feasibility’, ‘level of creativity', and ‘on brief”. With only one day left, feasible to prototype was an important factor.

Creating a USER FLOW to understand and empathize.

Trying to relive the pains of the creating and playing a music quizzes, our solution focused on creating a smooth of a user flow as possible. By mapping out all the different steps of the user journey, we made sure that the reward for going through each section was higher than the investments. Guided by our user flow, the different frames was created.

High Fidelity Prototype using Figma

Guided by the user flow, frame by frame came to life in Figma so the user could walk smoothly through the journey within the prototype. By carefully placing the feature in the app - hidden as a gem - we targeted the personas identified by Spotify in the brief.

The user flow was our blueprint for building the prototype so it felt intuitive and straightforward to navigate. By following this structure, users could use the prototype to learn about how the new features would work in practice.

Hiding the feature as a gem

We strategically placed the feature as a hidden gem in the app. This In order to create an exclusive feel, hoping to create excitement for the target group - ’the Social Trendsetters' and 'Master Curators’ - personas identified by Spotify. This placement also ensured that the Quiz doesn’t distract the person that only want to use Spotify for streaming.

The Quiz

Try to beat Nils Mozart

The user first create the questions that they want to have in the quiz. The user can make questions from songs, artists, title, year, album or make their own custom questions. Next they chose how many options they want for each question and add in the options. They then start the quiz and a QR-code and numer code is generated. All participants can scan the quiz masters phone or manually type in the code (if the one participating is not physically close). The quiz then starts and the participants answers. As th quiz goes on the participants can see the scoreboard.

We tried to make our client to feel as involved as possible. We now hade agreed on a set of delivrebles, and by starting to and asking Associative questions like "How does Hubista sound?", using their answers in our creative process.

2.2 Define

Our challenge now was to create a concept that is easy and straightforward when usability is key, but that also acts as a stage for personal stories and expression when inspiration is needed the most. A system that is both Inspirational as well as a Trustworthy. We solved this challenge by creating two modes; From simple to expressive. This allowed us to dial our designs up and down, still being true to our Creative Direction.

The last step before designing, was to agree on a list all the core elements of the Hubista. This would serve as a 'design toolbox' that includes all the brand elements necessary for a comprehensive design process.

03. Produce the Design

3.1 Develop

Our commitment to having close communication between the client and our design team ensured that every member of the team was able to see the vision clearly. This meant that everyone brought their own creativity and hard work to the mix, and because of that we were able to dig into the work of creating the different parts of the toolbox quickly and efficiently.


3.2 Build

The unification of the strategy, concept, and design that emphasizes the client’s key messages was at the core of this project. It all resulted in a compiled Brand Book for our client - a guide on all aspects of the design, and how the different parts worked together into the final identity.
