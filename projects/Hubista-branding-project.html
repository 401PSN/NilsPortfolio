Hubista Branding Project

CHALLENGE

Hubista is an upcoming marketplace that matches people with talent to those willing to pay for it. They hired our team from Hyper Island to assess, develop and bringing their business idea into life, by designing a Brand Identity.

PARTICIPANTS Elton GutochDesigner & Lead Producer Nils AnderssonDesigner & Producer Jasmine ArgaviCopywriter & Producer Rui BastosDesigner & Producer Maryam GhavdelDesigner & Producer
01. Set a Strategy

1.1 Learn

For this project, we began with extensive research, including question and answer meetings with our client to determine their goals for the project. In addition to gathering valuable information about their mission and strategy, this approach allowed us to identify the product's key areas of refinement in advance of creating the Brand Identity.

We wanted our client to feel involved during this part, and the goal for our was to learn as much as possible of Hubistas core. The business, their world, and the opportunities of the brand.

1.2 Identify

We used a combination of desk research and insights from conversations with people in the target group to narrow down categories of services they wanted to focus on, refining their value proposition, and giving them tasks to further define parts of their business plan. We ended this part of the process by creating a Creative Brief. The intention of it was to fully align on what Hubista is and should be. This in order to build the brand on top of this foundation - always having something to refer to, and back up future design decisions.

We narrowed Hubistaâ€™s target audience by converting all of the personas into to two main groups, Sellers and Buyers. From there, we wrote two job stories for each group. This allowed us to do less assumptions and more focus on the drivers and the motivation behind our target group.

Position first, design later

After reviewing our findings in the initial phase, our team understood that being able to move forward, we needed to position Hubista in the market. Deciding on on how wide, or narrow they wanted to go was a crucial step before starting working on our deliverables. We solved this together with the client by giving them three different options, called Creative Directions.

1.3 Aim

Each proposed Direction contained a draft of a Brand Narrative, a Brand Principle, Pros / Cons, and a short summery on 'How to make it work'. This was valuable for both our team to get a overview of the potential in the different approaches, but also for our client to understand that design for everyone is to design for no one.

By thoroughly understanding Hubistaâ€™s values, vision, and business personality, our team and the client were able to successfully decide on direction a that encompassed all of these elements. We were now ready to move over to the next step of the process - the Design Concept.

02. Creating a Concept

2.1 Explore

We started exploring ways to transform our direction into a visual identity. By experimenting with mood boards, stylescapes, animations and sketches, we managed to source a lot of inspiration for a unique visual identity.

We tried to make our client to feel as involved as possible, asking Associative questions like "How does Hubista sound?", using their answers in our creative process.

2.2 Define

We focus on concept and tonality, and client should be inspired.

Brand Narrative

People want to be inspired. It's what helps us keep up with change; what motivates us to be and do better. But in a fast-paced world full of options, finding that extra sparkle can be tricky. Hubista understands that the world is changing fast and that people's needs are different every day. It strives to give people the chance to inspire and be inspired. The place to be when life needs to be vibrant. Hubista makes life exciting again.
