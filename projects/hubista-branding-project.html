Hubista Branding Project

CHALLENGE

Hubista is an upcoming marketplace that matches people with talent to those willing to pay for it. They hired our team from Hyper Island to assess, develop, and bring their business idea to life, by designing a Brand Identity.

Duration: 4 Weeks
PARTICIPANTS Rui BastosProject Manager Nils AnderssonDigital Designer Elton GutochDesigner & Producer Jasmine AgdamiCopywriter & Designer Maryam GhavidelDesigner
01. Set a Strategy

1.1 Learn

For this project, we began with extensive research, including question and answer meetings with the client to determine their goals for the project. In addition to gathering valuable information about their mission and strategy, this approach allowed us to identify the product's key areas of refinement in advance of creating the Brand Identity.

We wanted our client to feel involved during this part, and the goal for us was to learn as much as possible about Hubistas core. The business, their world, and the opportunities of the brand.

1.2 Identify

We used a combination of desk research and insights from conversations with people in the target group to narrow down categories of services they wanted to focus on, refining their value proposition, and giving them tasks to further define parts of their business plan. We ended this part of the process by creating a Creative Brief. This approach intended was to first fully align on what Hubista is and should be, and then build the brand on top of this foundation - always having something to refer to and back up future design decisions.

We narrowed Hubistaâ€™s target audience by converting all of the personas into two main groups, Sellers and Buyers. From there, we wrote two job stories for each group. This allowed us to do fewer assumptions and more focus on the drivers and the motivation behind our target group.

After reviewing our findings in the initial phase, our team understood that to be able to move forward, we needed to position Hubista in the market. Deciding on how wide, or narrow they wanted to go was a crucial step before starting working on our deliverables. We solved this together with the client by giving them three different options, called Creative Directions.

1.3 Aim

Each proposed Direction contained a draft of a Brand Narrative, a Brand Principle, Pros / Cons, and a summary on 'How to make it work'. This was valuable for both our team to get an overview of the potential in the different approaches, but also for our client to understand that design for everyone is to design for no one.

By thoroughly understanding Hubistaâ€™s values, vision, and business personality, our team and the client were able to successfully decide on the direction that encompassed all of these elements. We were now ready to move over to the next step of the process - the Design Concept.

02. Creating a Concept

2.1 Explore

We started exploring ways to transform our direction into a visual identity. By experimenting with mood boards, stylescapes, animations, and sketches, we managed to source a lot of inspiration for a unique visual identity.

We tried to make our client feel as involved as possible. We now had agreed on a set of deliverables, and by starting to and asking Associative questions like "How does Hubista sound?", using their answers in our creative process.

2.2 Define

Our challenge now was to create a concept that is straightforward when usability is key, but that also acts as a stage for personal stories and expression when inspiration is needed the most. A system that is both Inspirational as well as a Trustworthy. We solved this challenge by creating two modes; From simple to expressive. This allowed us to dial our designs up and down, still being true to our Creative Direction.

The last step before designing was to agree on a list of all the core elements of the Hubista. This would serve as a 'design toolbox' that includes all the brand elements necessary for a comprehensive design process.

03. Produce the Design

3.1 Develop

Our commitment to having close communication between the client and our design team ensured that every member of the team was able to see the vision. This meant that everyone brought their creativity and hard work to the mix, and because of that, we were able to dig into the work of creating the different parts of the toolbox quickly and efficiently.


3.2 Build

The unification of the strategy, concept, and design that emphasizes the clientâ€™s key messages were at the core of this project. It all resulted in a compiled Brand Book for our client - a guide on all aspects of the design, and how the different parts worked together into the final identity.
