Spotify Music Quiz

CHALLENGE

The UX/UI challenge, as given by D&AD - Young Blood Awards, was to get listeners to "soundtrack" and share their life through Spotify. Our team was to build a feature within the app to help people easily share their favorite music and get people talking. And this is how we did it.

Duration: 48h
PARTICIPANTS Nils Andersson Project Manager Hanna LarssonUx Designer Anne ObelCopywriter Johanna LööfGraphic Designer
Creating a seamless Music Quiz experience

Understanding that people are eager to party again, we wanted to create something where humans can engage with one and other, through the power of music.

Looking for pent-up demand and needs due to covid; jobs-to-be-done sharing music; and pains & gains doing so - we found something that everybody loves to do, but only does once in a while. And we asked ourselves the question - why?

Everybody loves Music Quizzes - except one person.

Playing a Music quiz is a lovely social, but the hustle of creating one; the uncle that always cheats; and spending hours decipher unreadable writing style is not as great.

A pain our team was excited to solve. Other ideas were turned downed after an intense ideating and scoring each idea in ‘prototyping feasibility’, ‘level of creativity', and ‘on brief”. With only one day left, feasible to prototype was an important factor.

Creating a USER FLOW to understand and empathize.

Trying to relieve the pains of creating and playing a music quiz, our solution focused on creating a smooth a user flow as possible. By mapping out all the different steps of the user journey, we made sure that the reward for going through each section was higher than the investments. Guided by our user flow, the different frames were created.

High Fidelity Prototype using Figma

Guided by the user flow, frame by frame came to life in Figma so the user could walk smoothly through the journey within the prototype. By carefully placing the feature in the app - hidden as a gem - we targeted the personas identified by Spotify in the brief.

The user flow was our blueprint for building the prototype so it felt intuitive and straightforward to navigate. By following this structure, users could use the prototype to learn about how the new features would work in practice.

Hiding the feature as a gem

We strategically placed the feature as a hidden gem in the app. This in a way to create an exclusive feel, hoping to create excitement for the target group - ’the Social Trendsetters' and 'Master Curators’ - personas identified by Spotify. This placement also ensured that the Quiz doesn’t distract the person that only wants to use Spotify for streaming.

The Quiz

By using Spotify's extensive database, adding trivia questions should be a fun and fast process. Choose alternatives, or spice up the quiz with a custom question. In a hurry? By navigating to the library, you can find a curated quiz generated by you, play a quiz that a friend has made.

Let the game begin!

The host controls the game from their screen and can keep track of who is in the lead. Meanwhile, the players are trying to figure out the correct answers. In the end, a winner will be crowned. By creating and sharing playlists people can get to know each other in a fun and innovative way.

Sharing

The threshold to share should be as low as possible. An automated personal design will be featured after completing the quiz, promoting either the result of the quiz or the quiz itself.

Through the integrated interactive linking feature on Instagram, users can be directed straight to the quiz feature, creating organic social media growth.
