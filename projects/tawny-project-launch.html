Tawny AI Product Launch

CHALLENGE

TAWNY.ai is a pioneer in the field of modern, AI-powered emotion detection technology. Our team from Hyper Island helped create a rollout plan for their new feature, TAWNY Live, a tool that analyses human emotions in real-time, bringing the human element to virtual meetings.

Duration: 5 Weeks
PARTICIPANTS Andrea UrquietaProject Manager Nils AnderssonDesign & Strategy Lead Sofia WidströmDesigner & Strategist Vilma LingslundeCopywriter & Strategist
01. Discovery
"Welcome to the Future of Software"

In the first week of our project with Tawny Ai, we immersed ourselves in the brand, interviewing different stakeholders from various departments, which helped us to understand the brand. Additionally, we were able to test out some of their products and experience ourselves what this front-runner AI-powered company stands for.

Communicate technological expertise with a human touch

Understanding the power of the technology, and the benefits of it, we understood that one challenge would be to communicate the complex topic in a digestible way.

02. Define
Standards to ensure future consistency

After gaining empathy for the different stakeholders, we understood that providing Tawny's marketing team with a guide easy to use was essential.

With the focus to be simple, clear, and direct, we developed a Messaging Framework and Style Guide based on well-defined research to understand the client’s problems and design challenges they face. We did this to help the marketing team retain consistency when using external designers, a problem mentioned by Tawny.

03. Develop
From insights to a face mesh system.

Using insights from our survey and interviews, two motives surfaced as the target group's favorites. One was pictures of human faces, and the other one was ”face scanning” dots and nets. By playing around with layouts based on these insights, we discovered that we could use the grid of a ”face scan” to create different layouts, by zooming in on different parts. This way, we had a layout that was unique for each post, but still consistent, and represented the core of the company.

04. Deliver
Re-usable design and Implementation Plan

We added an Implementation Plan to the final deliverables, as well as a workshop in Figma. There we ran through how the mesh system would work, and how they could easily use our templates, making sure our deliverables were easy to take over by the marketing team.

Working on this brief was very enjoyable, and watching the deliverables being in use today, makes the whole project very rewarding.
