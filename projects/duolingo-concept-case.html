Duolingo Concept Case

CHALLENGE

The brief, as given by D&AD - New Blood Awards, was to figure out how to put Doulingo in the hands of 16-22 yeard olds all around the globe. This is how we did it.

Duration: 72h
PARTICIPANTS Nils Andersson Digital Producer Hanna LarssonDigital Producer
The Challenge
New Blood Awards

The brief, as given by D&AD - New Blood Awards was to figure out how to put Doulingo in the hands of 16-22 year olds all around the globe through an integrated campaign that drives them to download and use Duolingo. By weaving language into their daily routines Duolingo early on, language learning is more likely to stick with them.

Main Insights
A Day In The Life

Aiming to solve the brief in a sprint-format (limited time for conducting research), we went into discovery phase allowing each other to research on their own and then conduct a group session. From the information gathered, we mapped out a "regular" day in the life of a 16-22 year old - which is shown below. Inside the map of activities we then mapped out where in this day it would fit best for the target audience to have time practicing Duolingo.

Focusing on three clear touch points where there would be room for a new habit; (1) In the morning Social media routine just after waking up, (2) commute back and forth to school and (3) the afternoon and evening stream.

More Insights About the Target Group

In addition to the mapped out activities, we also gained knowledge about that the target group views of being knowledgeable is perceived as “cool”, they are passionate activists, and they see them-selves as more connected with culture than previous generations. They do not watch television, but consume a lot of content on other platforms and get their news from social media.

Problem Statement & Ideation
Creating an How Might We

After gathering this insights, together with the understanding of how a regular day looks like for the common 16-22 year old, we formulated a "How might we..”, and went into ideation.

How might we communicate changing an exciting habit into a new habit?

During this process, many ideas and angles solving the brief, but we chose to move forward with the idea that was not only very close to what Duolingo is known for, playing with words, but also related to many of our insights.

The Concept
Anagram an Habit into A New One

Duolingo is based on the foundation that learning should be fun. Our campaign reflects this playfulness of the brand by playing with language & words. Each ad is built on the same foundation, and each of them correlates to the different touch points we identified in the research.

Our idea was an integrated campaign targeted to 16-22 year olds where the message was that common habits such as watching Netflix, scrolling while waiting for the bus or suddenly energy peaks can be turned into language learning opportunities. We use anagrams as a representation of the transformation our target group want to see in themselves. By turning an existing habit into a more valuable one. All while using the quirky (almost passive-aggressive) undertone that Duo is known for.

Since teens view being knowledgable as cool we wanted to make the ads really clever and smart.

Marketing Mix
Channels

Based on our previous research we suggested Duolingo to run the campaign in several different channels where the target audience are spending their time and have time over for practicing Duolingo. The channels that we saw fit to run the campaign in were:

Video ads around the city, such as in bus stops for instance

Social media ads, mainly in TikTok, Instagram, Youtube and Snapchat
