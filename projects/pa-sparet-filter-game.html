På Spåret Filter Game

Challange

Transforming a classic quiz show into a digital experience. På Spåret Filter is packed with interactive moments, ready for a new generation to enjoy. This is how we did it:

Duration: 72 Hours
PARTICIPANTS Nils AnderssonDesign & Developer Sara LailasdotterDesign Lead Clara ÖhlingDesign & Copywriter Clara BaadeDesign & Copywriter
Align
Right down through the Fire

Once we began this project, we set up a process to make progress. We allocated roles and decided on a clear way of working together. Our approach was to create room for as many ideas as possible, and we decided on a brainstorming-and-voting approach.

Discover & Lab
Human Needs + Games = What?

Our design-driven approach started with human needs. To maximize our creative output, we used post-it, one with a game one with a human need combining them and turning them into new ideas. This is a way to come up with as many solutions as possible in a short amount of time.

Building on top of, discussing, and merging ideas, the team voted—ending up with three alternatives. By putting them into a value matrix, the team could make a decision on which one would move forward with.

Core Concept
Writing a killer hook

To make it concrete before prototyping, we distillate our thoughts into an idea description.

Idea Description.

Take the popular game show 'På Spåret' home and to your phone! This is an Instagram filter that tests your geographical knowledge. You get up to five clues ranging in difficulty. First clue too hard? Just tilt your head left. When you think you know the answer, tilt your head right and slam the breaks! Challenge your friends and family and send them the results!

Develop
Controlled Chaos Collaboration

To increase the speed at which the idea was created, assessed, and prioritized, we built the prototype in Figma the perfect tool for team collaboration. By quickly visualize and thoughtfully communicate, the visual design came to life.

As a parallel activity to the design team, our copywriters developed succinct and engaging questions, ensuring that the content was relevant, focus-group calibrated, and appropriate for the standard range of variables on the filter platform.

Build
Making the Filter

The prototype for the filter had been created with some basic functions. After the sprint, I took the task into my own hands and continued development by adding more complex features.

Through the use of Sparks Ar, various features were added – including a countdown clock, audio effects, several destinations, and more. My team shared my vision and contributed ideas which were now incorporated into a filter.

The design combines simplicity with a high degree of usability that has been tested extensively to ensure that it’s easy to use.
